The song confronts the listener with the uncomfortable truth that it’s in their own nature to be gripped watching other people’s gedy and suffering, hence the song’s name “vicarious”, which means “experienced as a result of watching [etc.] the activities of other people, rather than by doing the activities yourself”, a concept closely related to – and perhaps used as an euphemism for – voyeurism, which is also mentioned in the song. While seemingly immoral, this shouldn’t be surprising since humans, like other animals, have evolved to notice bad things first (e.g. the colour red as the colour of blood), because those who noticed the tasty fruit before the nearby corpse joined the corpse and left no offspring, but those who watched them die from a distance figured out a way to get the fruit safely. This is the ultimate learning from others' mistakes: the fascination that we feel is the nature’s way of holding our attention, while the feeling of safety and satisfaction is our reward. So don’t hide from yourself behind morality and say it with Keenan: “I need to watch things die”.
